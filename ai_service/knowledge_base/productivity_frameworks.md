# Timeblocking and Habits Optimization

## Timeblocking Strategy
Timeblocking allocates specific 24-hour vertical timeline grids for tasks. It prevents context switching and ensures dedicated execution.
- **Deep Focus blocks**: Schedule 90-120 minute blocks for complex coding, design, or learning tasks.
- **Buffer blocks**: Allocate 15-30 minute periods for emails, administrative reviews, and breaks.
- **Glowing Clock Indicator**: GrowthOS planner displays a live horizontal glow line representing current time coordinates. Align block starts with this line.

## Habit Loop Triggers (Atomic Habits)
A habit consists of four elements: Cue, Craving, Response, and Reward.
- **Habit Stacking**: Couple a new habit with an existing routine. Example: "After I brew morning coffee (Cue), I will review 5 Spaced Repetition flashcards (Response)."
- **Streak Freezes**: Consistency is key, but fatigue is real. Redeeming a Streak Freeze (costing 200 XP) prevents your daily streak multiplier from resetting when missing check-ins.

## Deep Work Execution
Cal Newport defines Deep Work as focus without distraction on cognitively demanding tasks. Keep the Focus Room timer running, play synthesized brown noise to mask distracting ambient sounds, and close secondary messaging channels.
